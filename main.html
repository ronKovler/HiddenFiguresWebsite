Follow through the story of a single mother, who prevailed against all odds in a workplace dominated by white men in the early 1960's. Katherine Johnson, former school teacher, embraces her new role as a "Human Computer" working at NASA's Langley building. There, Katherine Johnson and her two fellow coworkers Mary Jackson and Dorothy Vaughan. One day during their daily assignments, Katherine is picked to be a designated calculator for the main NASA team working on the FriendShip 7 Rocket. While there, she faces an ongoing struggle. Her fellow coworkers treat her as a second class citizen. Meanwhile, Mary Jackson has to persevere against segregation laws to finish school and receive her Enginnering Degree. Dorothy herself struggles being a "makeshift" supervisor for the west computing group and fights to earn the title of supervisor. As her days as the designated calculator continue, Katherines expertise begins to shine, while at the same time the racial preassure expands.
